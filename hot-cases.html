Hot SMSF Court Cases

Merchant v Commissioner of Taxation [2025]

In April 2025, the Full Court of the Federal Court of Australia ruled on the Merchant case, a significant decision for SMSFs despite not directly involving one. The Gordon Merchant Superannuation Fund purchased listed securities worth $5.8 million from the related Merchant Family Trust (MFT) in 2015, crystallizing a $56.5 million capital loss for MFT. This loss offset a capital gain from another MFT transaction. The ATO argued the transaction’s dominant purpose was to obtain a tax benefit, applying Part IVA of the Income Tax Assessment Act 1936. The ATO also alleged breaches of the SIS Act, including the sole purpose test (s 62), operating standards (s 34), and financial assistance rules (s 65), leading to Mr. Merchant’s disqualification as a trustee. The Administrative Appeals Tribunal (AAT) overturned the disqualification, citing Mr. Merchant’s low risk of future non-compliance and lack of prior warnings from advisers. However, the Full Court upheld (2:1) the tax benefit ruling, emphasizing that even routine transactions like acquiring listed securities at market value can attract scrutiny if tax avoidance is suspected. This case highlights the need for SMSF trustees to thoroughly assess transactions with related parties, ensuring compliance with superannuation laws to avoid severe tax and regulatory consequences.
