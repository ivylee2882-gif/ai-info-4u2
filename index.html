History of SMSFs

Self-Managed Super Funds (SMSFs) were introduced in Australia in the 1990s as a flexible retirement savings option. Governed by the Superannuation Industry (Supervision) Act 1993 (SIS Act), SMSFs allow members to act as trustees, giving them control over investment decisions. Over the years, SMSFs have grown in popularity, with over 600,000 funds managing $800 billion in assets by 2025, according to the ATO. They offer tailored investment strategies but come with strict compliance requirements enforced by the ATO.

Roles of Main Parties and Regulators

  • ATO: The Australian Taxation Office regulates SMSFs, ensuring compliance with super and tax laws, conducting audits, and enforcing penalties for breaches. ATO Regulation
  • APRA (Small APRA Funds): The Australian Prudential Regulation Authority oversees small APRA funds, an alternative to SMSFs, with professional trustees for those preferring less responsibility.
  • SMSF Association: A key industry body providing education, advocacy, and guidance to trustees and professionals. SMSF Association
